Information About Us

One summer, a particularly memorable trip to a remote lake in the mountains sparked the idea for this project. A group of us had planned a weekend getaway, but the popular campsites were fully booked. Determined not to let this ruin our plans, we decided to venture off the beaten path. After a long hike, we stumbled upon an unmarked, pristine lake surrounded by towering trees and vibrant wildflowers. We spent the weekend swimming in the crystal-clear waters and camping under the stars, completely disconnected from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. This experience highlighted the joy of discovering and sharing lesser-known outdoor spots. Realizing that many other nature enthusiasts like myself might also benefit from a platform that connects them to these hidden gems, I decided to create the Global Wild Swimming and Camping Website. This project aims to build a community where people can share their favorite wild swimming spots and camping sites, complete with reviews, photos, and tips. It’s a way to help others find the same sense of adventure and tranquility that I have always found in the great outdoors. In addition to my personal experiences, this project also serves as a Portfolio Project for Holberton School. Holberton’s emphasis on real-world applications and hands-on learning provided the perfect environment to bring this idea to life. By combining my passion for nature with the skills and knowledge gained at Holberton, I am excited to create a platform that inspires and connects outdoor enthusiasts around the world. For more information about Holberton School and its innovative approach to education, you can visit their official website.
